# GreenSpark MFC Narrative Design

## Objective

Align the mandatory deliverables with the approved GreenSpark concept: a phased circular-energy proposal for Santa Cruz de la Sierra that begins with university-backed research and simulation of microbial fuel cell (MFC) reactors and can later scale toward biodigesters.

## Approved Scope

### Phase 1: Research and simulation

- Initial paying clients: private universities.
- Deliverable: researched MFC reactor design, simulation, sustainability dashboard and AI-assisted scenario analysis.
- Purpose: validate substrates, operating variables, projected electrical output and potential agronomic recovery before claiming commercial performance.

### Phase 2: MFC pilots

- Potential clients: private schools, restaurants and agroindustrial companies.
- Deliverable: modular MFC pilots with measurement, monitoring and sustainability metrics.
- Energy goal: explore contribution of up to 10% for selected loads, subject to validation with measured data.

### Phase 3: Capacity scaling

- Potential technology: biodigesters or hybrid configurations when sufficient separated bioorganic residue is available.
- Energy goal: project contribution of up to 20% for selected loads, subject to feasibility studies and measured baselines.
- Economic opportunity: evaluate self-consumption first and regulated compensation for eligible distributed-generation surpluses afterward.

## Technical Honesty Rules

- The hackathon MVP is a researched and simulated MFC design, not a physical reactor.
- MFC electrical output must be described as projected until a physical pilot measures it.
- The residual material is a subproduct with agronomic potential subject to characterization, stabilization and applicable regulation. It is not automatically a market-ready fertilizer.
- The 10% and 20% values are exploratory goals, not guaranteed savings.
- Distributed generation must cite the current Bolivian regulatory sequence: DS 4477, modified by DS 5167 and DS 5549.

## Useful AI

- Predict projected reactor performance from substrate and operating variables.
- Compare scenarios and recommend configurations for future experiments.
- Detect simulated or measured anomalies when pilot data becomes available.
- Explain results and produce traceable sustainability reports without inventing figures.

## Business Logic

- Phase 1: paid research agreements and institutional innovation programs.
- Phase 2: MFC pilot implementation, monitoring and sustainability reporting.
- Phase 3: larger circular-energy projects, including biodigester feasibility assessments and regulated distributed-generation opportunities.

## Documents To Align

- Numbered mandatory documents: problem, PESTEL, FODA and proposed solution.
- Cross-cutting documents: technical research, AI application, architecture, Lean Canvas, financial analysis and expected impact.
